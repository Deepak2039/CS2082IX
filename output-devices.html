1.6 Output Devices

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the purpose and types of output devices
  • Learn about different display technologies (LED, LCD)
  • Compare various types of printers and their uses
  • Understand common computer ports and their functions

1.6.1 Overview of Output Devices

Output devices are hardware components that display or present data processed by a computer in a human-readable form. They convert electronic data into a form that users can understand, such as text, images, sounds, or physical output.

Categories of Output Devices

Display Devices

Show visual output (e.g., monitors, projectors)

Printers

Produce hard copy output (e.g., laser, inkjet, 3D printers)

Audio Devices

Produce sound output (e.g., speakers, headphones)

Other Output

Projectors, plotters, and more

1.6.2 Display Units

Display units are the most common output devices that present visual information from the computer.

LED Monitor

  • Technology: Light Emitting Diodes
  • Advantages: Energy efficient, thin design, better contrast
  • Uses: Computer monitors, TVs, digital signage
  • Lifespan: 50,000-100,000 hours

LCD Monitor

  • Technology: Liquid Crystal Display with CCFL backlight
  • Advantages: Lightweight, less power consumption than CRT
  • Uses: Computer monitors, laptops, tablets
  • Resolution: Ranges from HD to 8K

LED vs LCD: Key Differences

LED Monitors
  • Use light-emitting diodes for backlighting
  • Better contrast and black levels
  • More energy efficient
  • Thinner and lighter
  • Longer lifespan
LCD Monitors
  • Use CCFL (cold cathode fluorescent) backlighting
  • Less expensive than LED
  • Thicker and heavier
  • Shorter lifespan than LED
  • More power consumption

1.6.3 Printers

Printers produce hard copies of digital documents and images on paper or other media.

Laser Printer

  • Technology: Uses laser beam and toner
  • Speed: 20-100+ pages per minute
  • Quality: High quality text, good for graphics
  • Best for: Office use, high-volume printing
  • Cost: Higher initial cost, lower cost per page

Ink-jet Printer

  • Technology: Sprays tiny droplets of ink
  • Speed: 5-35 pages per minute
  • Quality: Excellent for photos and color printing
  • Best for: Home use, photo printing
  • Cost: Lower initial cost, higher cost per page

Dot-matrix Printer

  • Technology: Uses pins to strike an inked ribbon
  • Speed: 50-500 characters per second
  • Quality: Low to medium, produces dot patterns
  • Best for: Multi-part forms, invoices, receipts
  • Specialty: Can create carbon copies

3D Printer

  • Technology: Adds material layer by layer
  • Materials: PLA, ABS, resin, metal, etc.
  • Uses: Prototyping, manufacturing, medical
  • Types: FDM, SLA, SLS, DLP
  • Applications: Product design, education, healthcare

1.6.4 Computer Ports

Ports are physical docking points that allow connection of external devices to a computer.

USB/Type-C

  • Versions: USB 1.0 to USB4
  • Speed: 12 Mbps (USB 1.1) to 40 Gbps (USB4)
  • Uses: Data transfer, charging, peripherals
  • Type-C Features: Reversible, supports video/audio, fast charging

HDMI

  • Versions: 1.0 to 2.1
  • Max Resolution: Up to 10K @ 120Hz
  • Uses: Audio/video transmission
  • Features: Carries audio and video, supports 3D, Ethernet

VGA

  • Type: Analog video
  • Max Resolution: 2048×1536 @ 85Hz
  • Uses: Older monitors, projectors
  • Status: Legacy, being phased out

Ethernet (RJ-45)

  • Speeds: 10/100/1000/10000 Mbps
  • Uses: Wired network connections
  • Advantages: Stable, secure, high speed
  • Types: Cat5e, Cat6, Cat7, Cat8

Audio Jack (3.5mm)

  • Types: TRS (stereo), TRRS (with mic)
  • Uses: Headphones, speakers, microphones
  • Colors: Green (audio out), Pink (mic in), Blue (line in)
  • Status: Being replaced by USB-C/Wireless

Summary

  • Output devices present processed data in human-readable form, including visual, audio, and physical outputs.
  • Display technologies like LED and LCD monitors provide visual output with different characteristics in terms of power consumption, contrast, and image quality.
  • Printers vary from laser (fast, high-volume) to inkjet (high-quality color) to dot-matrix (impact printing for forms) and 3D printers (additive manufacturing).
  • Computer ports like USB/Type-C, HDMI, VGA, Ethernet, and audio jacks enable connection of various output and input devices to computers.
  • Modern interfaces like USB-C and wireless technologies are gradually replacing older port standards.
